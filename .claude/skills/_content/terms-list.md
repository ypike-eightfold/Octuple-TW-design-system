## Guides hierarchy

- Follow content design standards and this terms list for in-product content
- For external marketing and communications, use the Eightfold brand guidelines
- For product documentation and knowledge base articles, use the Eightfold docs style guide
- For any topic not covered in this style guide, default to using the Microsoft Style Guide

## Sensitive terms

These terms have a higher potential to cause harm to individuals, communities, and Eightfold as a brand. To minimize risk, adhere to the following guidance.

### Race and ethnicity

Only refer to race and ethnicity when it is legally necessary or refers directly to a user control.

- Be as specific as possible
- If necessary, use specific categories defined by the government. In the US, this would be the definitions provided by the EEOC and NIH.
- Government definitions may not apply globally.
- Always get legal review and feedback
- Do not use POC (person of color)

### Workforce categories

**Do not use:**
- Human capital

**Use with caution:**
- Blue collar
- Skilled and unskilled labor

## Glossary

### A

#### Add
- **Use:** "Add" in context of where info will be saved
- **Don't use:** (no specific incorrect form)
- **Capitalization:** sentence case
- **Definition:** Enter info and save to a specific location
- **Notes:**
  - Clear action; shorter and simpler than "enter"
  - Use with caution: "Add to" specifies location. Confirm accuracy — if info is "added" somewhere, people expect to access it there. Check UX value: naming a location does not mean people know what it is or how to find it.
  - MDI icon: `plus`
- **Related:** Save

#### Advance
- **Use:** "Advance stage" (CTA to move candidate to next stage); "<name> advanced to <stage name>"; "<name> advanced to <stage name> in <pipeline name>"
- **Don't use:** "Advance Stage"; "<name> advanced successfully to <stage name>"
- **Capitalization:** sentence case
- **Definition:** Specific action taken by recruiter to move a candidate forward in the hiring process
- **Notes:** Only add pipeline name info when it is useful to the user.

#### AI
- **Use:** AI (acronym)
- **Capitalization:** always capitalized
- **Definition:** Acronym for "artificial intelligence". Broadly used and understood, so ok to use.
- **Notes:**
  - MDI icon `creation`: indicates AI available or in use (general)
  - MDI icon `clock-star-four-points-outline`: indicates AI available or in use (related to time)
  - MDI icon `checkbox-marked-circle-auto-outline`: indicates AI available or in use (related to a checklist or tasks)

#### AI agent
- **Use:** AI agent
- **Don't use:** AI Agent; AI recruiter; AI Recruiter
- **Capitalization:** sentence case
- **Definition:** General term for AI that performs specific tasks for the user
- **Notes:** Marked with caution sign. Can be used in all agentic AI experiences.
- **Related:** AI Companion, AI Interviewer, AI Recruiter

#### AI Companion
- **Use:** "AI Companion" (refer to feature by name); "AI Companion added" (when feature has been added to a specific interview); "Eightfold AI Companion" (only on video calls, to differentiate from Zoom and Google Meet companions)
- **Don't use:** "AI companion" (lowercase); "Eightfold's AI Companion" (possessive form)
- **Capitalization:** always capitalized (branded term)
- **Definition:** Name for feature within AI Interviewer that transcribes interviews (candidate + recruiter), summarizes notes (candidate + recruiter), and makes suggestions in the interview feedback form (recruiter).
- **Notes:**
  - This name is based on P0 capabilities: text-based functionality only (analysis).
  - If audio or video recording are added, team must re-evaluate if name satisfies transparency and usability requirements.
  - Do not use Eightfold brand name anywhere else in product flow.

#### AI interview
- **Use:** "This screening round will be an AI interview" (in product, referring to specific interviews)
- **Don't use:** AI Interview (capitalized) when referring to specific interviews
- **Capitalization:** sentence case
- **Definition:** Interview conducted by an AI agent
- **Notes:**
  - In product, use "AI interview" (not capitalized) to refer to specific interviews.
  - Use "AI Interviewer" (capitalized) only when referring to the feature.

#### AI interview guide
- **Use:** AI interview guide
- **Don't use:** AI Interview Guide
- **Capitalization:** sentence case
- **Definition:** Structured instructions for an AI interview with basic details, interview questions, and settings.
- **Notes:** Guides can be saved and reused for different positions.

#### AI Interviewer
- **Use:** "Announcing Eightfold AI Interviewer" (only when referring to the feature); "Ask AI Interviewer" (CTA to launch feature)
- **Don't use:** AI Recruiter (internal use only)
- **Capitalization:** always capitalized (branded product name)
- **Definition:** Product name for agentic AI that conducts interviews.
- **Notes:**
  - Use AI Interviewer (capitalized) only when referring to the product.
  - In product, use "AI interview" (not capitalized) to refer to specific interviews.
  - In product, use "AI interviewer" (not capitalized) to refer to the agent itself.

#### AI interviewer
- **Use:** AI interviewer (lowercase)
- **Capitalization:** sentence case
- **Definition:** General term for agentic AI that conducts interviews.
- **Notes:**
  - Do not capitalize when referring to the AI agent in product.
  - Capitalize AI Interviewer when referring to the product.

#### AI interview score
- **Definition:**
  - Assessment of a candidate's performance during an AI interview. Use only for AI Interviewer (TA).
  - Sub-component of Match score. Does not exist before candidate takes an AI interview.
  - A candidate's Match score will be recalculated each time an AI interview score is added as a sub-component.
- **Notes:**
  - Use AI Interviewer branding to communicate that an AI interview score is available.
  - Use in context of Match Score.

#### AI Recruiter
- **Use:** Do not use in product.
- **Capitalization:** N/A
- **Notes:**
  - Marked with stop sign — internal use only.
  - This is how we refer to the broad initiative of building multiple AI agents for recruiter workflows (TA).
- **Related:** AI Interviewer

#### AI suggestion
- **Capitalization:** sentence case
- **Notes:**
  - Marked with caution sign.
  - Label content created by generative AI.
  - Do not show label after user has reviewed and accepted the suggestion. Reason: the content is now owned by user / conscious decision has been made.
- **Resources:** EU AI Act (article 50); Octuple guidance for AI logo and icon usage.

#### And
- **Use:** and (spell out the word)
- **Don't use:** &
- **Notes:** Do not use "&" symbol in UI — causes browser and translation bugs.

#### Anonymous, Anonymize
- **Use:** Do not use without legal review.
- **Notes:**
  - Marked with stop sign — legal review required.
  - Under privacy laws, to "anonymize" means to eliminate all personal data such that the person can no longer be re-identified.
  - Be specific and descriptive about what the backend is actually doing with the data.
  - Try: mask, won't show, hide, remove identifying information. These are options to try, but not pre-approved. Legal approval required.

#### Applicant
- **Use:** applicant (noun)
- **Capitalization:** sentence case
- **Definition:** A person, internal or external, who submits their application for a role within a company.
- **Notes:**
  - Use to describe a person who applied to a job.
  - Don't use if another term is more specific about the stage of a person's job search (see Candidate, Prospect, Lead).
  - Don't use in flows for career builders (job seekers).
- **Product area:** Talent Experience (PCS), Talent Acquisition. Audience: recruiters, hiring managers.

#### Apply
- **Use:** Use to describe applying for a job.
- **Don't use:** Don't use to describe applying changes.
- **Definition:** The action a person takes to submit an application for a role within a company.
- **Related:** Save, Submit

#### Approver
- **Definition:** Person involved in the review and validation of job requisitions and offer details during the hiring process. They can add comments, request changes, and provide approval to advance the requisition or offer to the next stage in the workflow.
- **Notes:** Different from Signer: Approvers ensure accuracy and compliance throughout the workflow, whereas Signers are solely responsible for legally signing the finalized offer.

#### Assess, Assessment
- **Use:** Skill assessment; Request assessment; Manage assessment; Edit assessment; Self-assessment (or self-assessment mid-sentence)
- **Don't use:** Skill Assessment; Request Assessment; Manage Assessment; Edit Assessment; Self Assessment
- **Capitalization:** sentence case
- **Definition:** Evaluate for quality or ability. Example: Assess a person's proficiency for a specific skill.
- **Notes:**
  - Use when there is rigor to the evaluation. For example, a manager would assess an employee's skill based on their job performance.
  - Use "endorse" when there is no rigor.
- **Related:** Endorse

#### Assign
- **Use:** "Assign" (CTA); "Unassign" (CTA to remove assignment)
- **Don't use:** Make assignment; UnAssign; Un-assign
- **Definition:** The act of allocating a task, resource, or responsibility to someone else.
- **Notes:** Context must clarify what is being assigned and who it is being assigned to.

#### Assistant
- **Use:** Do not use without UX alignment.
- **Notes:**
  - Marked with stop sign.
  - Definition: A product or feature that performs one or more complex tasks, like respond to voice commands, interpret and answer questions, make calls, play music, or order products.
  - Noun.
  - This term sets high expectations, so a product must meet or exceed expectations to prevent a negative user experience.
  - To qualify for this term, a product or feature must: save people time or energy; offer a technologically advanced experience.

#### Auto
- **Definition:** Setting for platform to take action based on specified criteria.
- **Notes:** Usually provided as an option, along with Manual.

#### Automate, Automated, Automation
- **Notes:**
  - Marked with caution sign.
  - Requirement: Get legal review if you are building something that combines AI with automation.

#### Autosave, Autosaved, Autosaving
- **Use:** "Autosaved" (use in simpler forms or when space is small); "All changes autosaved" (use only when precise time or date is not necessary); "Autosaving all changes…" (use when saving is in progress, include ellipsis); "Autosaved {time}" (assumes date is clear from context); "Autosaved {time} {date}"; "Autosaved {time elapsed}"
- **Don't use:** Auto-save; Auto save; All changes auto-saved; All changes are autosaved; Auto-saving all changes; Autosaving your changes (missing ellipsis); Autosaved at {time}; Autosaved at {time} on {date}
- **Capitalization:** one word, no hyphen
- **Notes:**
  - One word, no hyphen.
  - No filler words ("at", "on").
  - Be specific: do not state that "all changes" are saved. There is a window between user changes and system save when this statement is inaccurate. Instead, specify time and date.
  - Use processing text or icon when action is not yet complete (see "Autosaving" in content patterns).
  - Show "Autosaved" only when changes have just autosaved. Do not show when there are unsaved changes.
- **Related:** Save, Updated, Last updated

### C

#### Candidate
- **Use:** candidate (noun)
- **Capitalization:** sentence case
- **Definition:** A person that is under active consideration for a position.
- **Notes:**
  - Don't use for career builders (job seekers). "Candidate" is the hiring company's POV.
  - Related terms: Applicant (Candidate is preferred because more specific); Prospect, Lead (different meanings, but part of same pipeline).
- **Product area:** Talent Experience (PCS), Talent Acquisition. Audience: recruiters, hiring managers.

#### Capacity
- **Definition:** Amount of resource's bandwidth requested in a demand. Often measured in hours per week.
- **Usage:** Resource Management (RM)
- **Related:** Demand, Resource

#### Change
- **Use:** "Change template" (when one item is completely replaced by another item)
- **Don't use:** Change Template; Change email (use "Edit email" when the item is revised, not replaced entirely); Change settings (use "Manage settings" when the item is reviewed or revised, not replaced entirely)
- **Capitalization:** sentence case
- **Definition:** Replace entirely
- **Related:** Edit, Manage

#### Child, Children
- **Notes:**
  - Marked with caution sign.
  - Use only for technical flows, like Admin Console.
  - Use only when the intended user is familiar with the technical meaning of "parent" and "child".
- **Definition:** The terms "parent" and "child" refer to a hierarchy where the "parent" is a higher-level entity that contains the "child" or "children" elements.
- **Related:** Parent

#### Choose
- **Use:** "Choose project type" (decision requires strategy — complex); "Choose quantity" (open-ended set of options)
- **Definition:** Pick from options shown (complex). If decision is simple, use "Select". Also: pick from open-ended set of options.
- **Related:** Select

#### Click
- **Use:** Do not use.
- **Notes:**
  - Marked with stop sign.
  - Not device-agnostic: Assumes user is on desktop.
  - Not inclusive: People using assistive technology don't click to take an action.
  - Not future-proof: Product or design changes could make a UI element not clickable.
  - Instead: follow standards for CTA.
- **Related:** Tap, Standards for Gestures

#### Close
- **Use:** MDI icon `close`
- **Definition:** Non-destructive action like closing a window.

#### Comment
- **Use:** MDI icon `comment-add-outline` (indicates where user can add a comment); "Add comment" (user can add text to share information or opinions)
- **Notes:**
  - Expectation of discussion: other people can view and respond to comments.
  - If text is intended to be seen and responded to by others, use "comment".
  - If text is intended for personal use or documentation, use "note".
- **Related:** Note

#### Condition
- **Definition:** A rule that determines when an automated workflow or action should occur.
- **Notes:** Use to describe the criteria that must be met for a specific outcome (TA > Workflow Automation).
- **Related:** Trigger

#### Confirm
- **Definition:** Approve a choice or an action.
- **Notes:**
  - Generally used for critical actions such as deletion, modification, and more.
  - Ensure there is clarity in the action that is being confirmed. For example: use "Confirm file upload" that asks for confirmation and mentions a specific action, instead of "Upload". Use "Confirm change password" in a confirmation window when a user initiates password change.
  - Avoid confirming every action. Excessive use reduces its importance and users might skip necessary confirmations.
- **Related:** Save, Submit

#### Copilot
- **Use:** Copilot (branded term, proper noun, always capitalize)
- **Don't use:** copilot; Co-pilot; CoPilot; Co-Pilot; COPilot; Copilots (no plurals)
- **Capitalization:** always capitalized
- **Notes:**
  - Singular only, NO plurals. There is one Copilot product applied to multiple use cases.
  - The logo and text lockup for Copilot shows a lowercase name. This is the only time that lowercase is ok for this term.

#### Copy
- **Definition:** Capture information temporarily to use elsewhere.
- **Notes:**
  - Use "copy" when the user can "copy and paste" text or images.
  - Do not use "copy" when a new artifact will be created (see "duplicate").
- **Related:** Duplicate

#### CSV, .csv
- **Notes:** Refer to standards for file types, filenames, and extensions.
- **Content patterns:**
  - File type: CSV
  - Filename: `for_example.csv`
  - File extension: `.csv`
- **Examples:**
  - Yes: Upload CSV, `for_example.csv`
  - No: Upload csv, `for_example.CSV`

#### Current employee
- **Use:** Do not use.
- **Notes:**
  - Marked with stop sign.
  - "Employee" is clear enough.
- **Related:** Former employee

#### Delegate
- **Use:** "Allow admins to delegate" (active voice); "Delegate approvals", "Delegate workflows" (CTA); "Delegated to <name>" (past tense ok); "Delegated accounts"
- **Don't use:** Delegator; Delegating; Delegatee; do not create new variants
- **Definition:** Assign responsibility or authority to another person (verb, action). Also: a person acting for another person (noun).
- **Notes:** Ok to use "delegate" to mean both the action (ex: delegate approvals) and the assigned person (ex: approved by delegate). Evaluate UI for context and clarity.

#### Delegation
- **Definition:** Category name for delegated items, that includes Delegated to me and Delegated to others.

#### Delete
- **Use:** "Delete"; "Delete {subject}" (e.g., "Delete project") when needed for clarity; MDI icon `trash-can-outline`
- **Don't use:** Close; Delete Project (title case)
- **Capitalization:** sentence case
- **Definition:** Erasing of an item or data.
- **Notes:** Use when an item is to be removed permanently.

#### Demand
- **Use:** "Demands" (name of demands listing page); "Create demand" (CTA to set up a new request); "Archive demand" (CTA to save data after demand is no longer active); "Delete demand" (CTA to erase data); "My demands" / "All demands"; "Showing <number> demands" (optional: can bold dynamic element); "Demand manager"
- **Don't use:** Demands Listing; Create Demand; Archive Demand; Delete Demand; My Demands; All Demands; Showing <number> Demands; Demand Manager
- **Capitalization:** sentence case
- **Definition:** A request for a short-term resource. Can be calibrated, like a position. Includes information like Program, Project, Start date, End date, Capacity.
- **Usage:** Resource Management (RM)

#### Development plan
- **Use:** "Development plan" (title, beginning of sentence); "development plan" (middle of sentence); "upskilling" (only when referring to the broad concept of learning)
- **Don't use:** Development Plan; Dev plan; dev plan; upskilling plan
- **Capitalization:** sentence case
- **Definition:** An action plan to acquire new skills and make progress towards a goal. Can be created by employees and managers. Can include courses, projects, and tasks.
- **Examples:** "Create a development plan to upskill your team"; "Career Hub offers upskilling solutions, like courses and development plans"
- **Products:** Talent Management (TM) > Career Hub
- **Related:** Upskilling

#### Disable
- **Use:** "Disable" (can be used as a CTA); "Disable {feature name}" when clarity is needed (use sentence case)
- **Don't use:** Deactivate
- **Capitalization:** sentence case
- **Definition:** To make a feature or functionality unavailable for use.
- **Notes:**
  - Marked with caution sign.
  - Use "Disable" to describe the action a user takes to turn off a specific feature or setting. Consider simpler, less risky CTAs like: Turn off, Off (for a toggle design).
  - UI context must clearly indicate what is being disabled. If context does not clarify, text must clarify.
  - Clarify the consequences: users must understand what will happen and what they will no longer be able to do. Prevent bad surprises. Specify if data will be retained, deleted, or hidden.
  - Consider privacy implications when a user disables a feature. If disabling affects the visibility or processing of personal information, get legal review.
  - The word "disable" is sensitive because it can also refer to physical or mental impairment. Avoid using emotional language or assumptions. Be factual and focus on the functional impact. Review all text with this term and question if it can be misinterpreted as insensitive.
- **Related:** Enable

#### DOC, DOCX, .doc, .docx
- **Notes:** Refer to standards for file types, filenames, and extensions.
- **Content patterns:**
  - File type: DOC, DOCX
  - Filename: `for_example.doc`, `for_example.docx`
  - File extension: `.doc`, `.docx`
- **Examples:**
  - Yes: `for_example.doc`
  - No: `for_example.DOC`

#### Draft
- **Use:** "Draft" (tag); "Save draft" (CTA); "Edit draft" (CTA); "1 draft" / "<number> drafts" (meta text)
- **Don't use:** Saved; Saved draft; Publish draft; Save Draft; Edit Draft; 3 Drafts; Draft email (as a verb — use "Write email" or "Create email" instead)
- **Capitalization:** sentence case
- **Definition:** A version of content (such as an email, message, or document) that is in progress and not yet finalized or sent.
- **Notes:**
  - Use "Draft" to indicate content that is being created or edited but has not been published, sent, or submitted.
  - Clearly differentiate between a saved draft and sent/finalized content.
  - Use with other terms like "Save" or "Edit" when more clarity is needed.
  - Do not use "Publish" in CTAs that change content status — does not add value (context makes it clear what will be published) and creates cognitive load.

#### Duplicate
- **Definition:** Create a new artifact using an existing artifact.
- **Notes:**
  - Use "duplicate" when the user will create something new by clicking a button.
  - Do not use "duplicate" when the user is just copying and pasting (see "copy").
- **Related:** Copy

#### Duration
- **Definition:** Refers to the window of time that something exists or lasts.
- **Notes:** Use as title or tooltip text to clarify what the unit of time means.
- **Use cases:**
  - Interview: Length of time scheduled, or length of time an interview is expected to last. Example: "20 min". See standards for time abbreviations.
  - Project: Start and end dates for a project. Example: "Mar 20, 2025 - Apr 20, 2025". See standards for date formatting and date abbreviations.

### E

#### Edit
- **Use:** "Edit" (can be used as a CTA); "Edit {subject}" (when needed for clarity); MDI icon `pencil`
- **Don't use:** Change; Edit Assessments (title case)

- **Notes:**

- **Related:** Manage

#### Eightfold, Eightfold AI
- **Use:** Eightfold AI; Eightfold
- **Don't use:** eightfold.ai; 8fold; EF
- **Notes:**
  - Marked with caution sign.

  - Do not compete with customer/client branding.
  - Do not introduce confusion.

#### Email
- **Use:** MDI icon `email-outline`

#### Enable
- **Use:** "Enable" (can be used as a CTA); "Enable {feature name}" when clarity is needed (e.g., "Enable skills enhancement"); use sentence case
- **Don't use:** Activate (implies that something will happen immediately); Enable Skills Enhancement
- **Capitalization:** sentence case
- **Definition:** To make a feature or functionality available for use.
- **Notes:**
  - Use "Enable" to describe the action a user takes to turn on a specific feature or setting.
  - UI context must clearly indicate what is being enabled. If context does not clarify, text must clarify.
- **Related:** Disable

#### Endorse
- **Use:** "Endorse" (can be used as a CTA); "Endorse for this skill"; "Endorse a teammate"
- **Don't use:** Recommend for this skill; Vouch for teammate
- **Capitalization:** sentence case
- **Definition:** Add public support for a person. Can be used to show that a person has a specific skill.
- **Notes:**
  - Context must make it clear what user is endorsing.
  - Use when there is no structured evaluation. For example, users can endorse teammates for skills for any reason.
  - Use "assess" when there is rigor to the evaluation. For example, a manager would assess an employee's skill based on their job performance.
- **Product area and personas:** TM > Employee
- **Related:** Assess, Refer

#### Enhance
- **Use:** "Enhance skills" (CTA for employees to update their profiles based on their company's skill taxonomy)
- **Definition:** Improve the quality, effectiveness, or value of existing information.
- **Notes:** HRBP admin have different CTA to enable this feature for employees: "Enable skills enhancement".
- **Related:** Skills enhancement

#### Error
- **Use:** MDI icon `alert-circle`
- **Notes:** Accessibility requirement: Use error icon before error text message.

#### External
- **Notes:**
  - Marked with caution sign.
  - Only use when necessary to specify whether info is available outside of a company.
  - Complex term that changes in meaning based on context.
  - Relies on user understanding of what "internal" means. Ex: Does it mean internal to the client company or internal to a specific team?
  - Leads to more questions about the boundaries of "external". Ex: Does it mean public, or are there other privacy settings?
